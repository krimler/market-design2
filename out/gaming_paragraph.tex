\textit{Gaming-resistance verification (\Cref{thm:divergence} Part 1).} On
ACSIncome ($d=68$) and TREC ($d=384$), we simulate a gaming attacker with
manipulation budget $\delta_G$ set to the 90th percentile of distance-to-boundary
on a held-out pool. Over 200 trials with query budget 50, the attacker's success
rate under no abstention, coarse 30\% abstention, and boundary-localizing
abstention with $\delta = \delta_G$ is, respectively, 1.00/1.00/0.00 on
ACSIncome and 1.00/1.00/0.00 on TREC. The localizing protocol blocks gaming
attacks entirely on both datasets, matching \Cref{thm:divergence} Part 1.
The coarse protocol leaves gaming attacks essentially undefended.
